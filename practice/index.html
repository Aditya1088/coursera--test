Physics

The various types of forces in nature can be grouped in four categories :- (i) Gravitational Force (ii) Electromagnetic Force (iii) Nuclear Force (iv) Weak Force
Any two bodies attract each other by virtue of their masses and this attractive force is called as Gravitational force. The force of attraction between two point masses is F = (G*m1*m2)/r^2 where m1 and m2 are the masses of the particle and r is the distance between them and G is the universal constant having the value 6.67 * 10^-11 N-m^2/kg^2.
The electromagnetic force is a type of physical interaction that occurs between electrically charged particles. It acts between charged particles and is the combination of all magnetic and electrical forces. The electromagnetic force can be attractive or repulsive. The electromagntic force is find out by F = (k*q1*q2)/r^2 where q1 and q2 are charges on two particles respectively and k is a constant whose value is 9.0 * 10^9 N-m^2/C^2.
The force occured betwween nucleus of an atom other than electromagnetic and gravitational forces are known as nucleus force . This force is shot range but yet it is the strongest force in the world. The changes of atom during a radiation is also done by force which is different from other forces and this force is known as weak forces.
Newton's first law of motion states that if the (vector) sum of all the forces acting on a particle is zero then the particle remains unaccelarated (i.e., remains at rest or moveswith constant velocity)
Newton's second law of motion states that the accelaration of a particle as measured from an inertial frame is given by the (vector) sum of all the forces acting on the particlee divided by its mass.
Newton's third law of motion states that every action has equal and opposite reaction.
The unwillingness of a particle to change its state of state or of uniform motion along a straight line is called as inertia.
Friction is a force between two surfaces that are sliding, or trying to slide, across each other. For example, when you try to push a book along the floor, friction makes this difficult. Friction always works in the direction opposite to the direction in which the object is moving, or trying to move.
There are two types of frictional force (i) Static friction :- When two bodies do not slip each other , the force of friction is called static friction. (ii) Kinetic friction :- When two solid bodies slip over each other , the force of friction is called kinetic friction.
Force is an interaction between two object. When you pull or push some object you exert a force on it. Force is an vector quantity. The SI unit of force is Newton (N).
